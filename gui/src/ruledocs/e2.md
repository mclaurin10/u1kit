## E2 — Estimated layer time clamp

If plate-area × layer-height ÷ volumetric-speed puts most layers under `slow_down_layer_time`, warns that `slow_down_min_speed` will dominate actual print speed. Particularly relevant for small-scale prints with aggressive cooling minimums.

**Severity:** info — no fix; the user decides whether to raise vmax, lower the cooling minimum, or accept the clamp.

**Why this matters:** on a small plate (under ~50 mm square) with a 12 s cooling minimum, even maxed-out volumetric speed can't outrun the slow-down clamp. People often tune "faster" settings that have no effect because the minimum cooling time is the real bottleneck.
